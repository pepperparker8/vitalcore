Nervous System Recovery
Heart Rate Variability
—
HRV data not yet available. Log a morning reading to see your recovery status.
ⓘ Why this matters ▾
HRV measures how well your autonomic nervous system is recovering. Higher = better recovery capacity. Low HRV after hard training is normal; sustained low HRV signals overreaching or illness onset.
Training Fatigue
Acute Training Load (7-day)
—
How much stress your body absorbed this week.
ⓘ Why this matters ▾
ATL reflects recent training stress. High ATL = you've been working hard. It should rise with training blocks and fall during recovery weeks. If ATL stays high without recovery, performance declines.
How Fresh You Are
Training Stress Balance
—
Fitness minus fatigue — your race-readiness number.
ⓘ Why this matters ▾
TSB = CTL − ATL. Positive TSB means you're fresh and ready to perform. Negative TSB means you're carrying fatigue. Optimal race TSB is typically +5 to +20. Deeply negative = overtraining risk.
